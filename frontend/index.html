I’ve gained some weight over the years and am not in the best shape. If I’m being honest, I’m tired of this belly hanging over my pants; I know it’s not the sexiest look. Which is a bit of a hit to the ego, not to mention I just hate the feeling of my stomach rubbing against my belt buckle.

I wanted my strapping, young body back, and figured if I started eating better and went back to the gym, I could probably get back in shape fairly quickly.

Well, I was seriously mistaken. After an entire year of restrictive, bland food and grueling workouts, I felt like my body had not changed at all. I was utterly frustrated and ready to give up.

To make matters worse, my wife forced me to go to yet another boring dinner at her friend’s place. Great, I thought. Another night of trying to make small talk with people I don’t know and have nothing in common with.

As I walked around with my small plate of scraps (the only food allowed on my diet), the owner of the house, Jason, walked up to me:

“Don’t like the food? Or do you have allergies?”

“I’m just trying to get back in shape”.

Jason laughed, slapped me on the back, and said, “Don’t waste your time. Come with me.”

He pulled me by the shoulder to his upstairs bathroom, pushed the door open, and  pointed to a scale on the floor.

“There is no point in doing anything with regard to fitness and health without that!”

He laughed again (which was strange because I’m not much of a comedian) and said,
